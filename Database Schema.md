# DATABASE SCHEMA DESIGN

## ENTITY AND ATTRIBUTES

## creators
*Influencers and content creators across various platforms who produce promotional content for brand campaigns*

- creator_id (Primary Key)
- creator_name
- niche (Beauty, fitness, gaming, food, etc.)
- follower_count
- post_count

## platforms
*Digital platforms where creators publish campaign content*

- platform_id (Primary key)
- platform_name 

## creator_platforms
*A bridge table linking creators to the platforms they actively use*

- creator_id (Primary Key, Foreign Key)
- platform_id (Primary key, Foreign Key)

## parent_companies
*Organizations that own or manage one or more brands*

- company_id (Primary Key)
- company_name
- founding_date

## brands
*Distinct brand identities that are owned by parent companies*

- brand_id (Primary Key)
- brand_name
- company_id (Foreign Key)
- industry

## products
*Individual products/services offered by a brand*

- product_id (Primary Key)
- product_name
- brand_id (Foreign Key)

## campaigns
*Marketing initiatives designed to promote a brand or a specific product*

- campaign_id (Primary Key)
- brand_id (Foreign Key)
- product_id (Foreign Key, Nullable)
- campaign_name
- campaign_type
- campaign_goal
- start_date
- end_date
- budget

## collaborations
*A bridge table that represents the partnerships between campaigns and creators*

- collaboration_id (Primary Key)
- campaign_id (Foreign Key)
- creator_id (Foreign Key)
- content_type
- payment_amount

## collaboration_platforms
*A bridge table linking collaborations to the platforms where the campaign content is posted*

- collaboration_id (Primary Key, Foreign_key)
- platform_id (Primary Key, Foreign Key)

## performance_metrics
*Engagement and performance data generated by the campaign collaborations* 

- collaboration_id (Primary Key, Foreign Key)
- platform_id (Primary Key, Foreign Key)
- metric_date (Primary Key)
- impressions
- clicks
- likes
- shares
- comments


## BUSINESS DOMAIN AND RULES

There are various companies based in the U.S. and each company runs at least one campaign for new or existing products and brands

1. A parent company can own multiple brands, but each brand belongs to one parent company.
2. A brand can have multiple products, but each product belongs to only one brand.
3. A brand can run multiple campaigns but each campaign belongs to one brand.
4. A campaign can promote either a specific product or the brand itself.
5. A campaign collaborates with at least one creator, but often multiple creators.
6. A creator can operate on one or multiple platforms
7. A creator can participate in multiple campaigns.
8. A Campaign collaboration represents the partnerships between a Campaign and a creator
9. A collaboration can occur on one or multiple platforms.
10. A platform can host many collaborations.
11. Performance metrics track the engagement results for each collaboration on a specific platform.
